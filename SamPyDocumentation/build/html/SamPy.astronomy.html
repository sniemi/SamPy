SamPy.astronomy.conversions.RAandDECfromStandardCoordinates(data)[source]¶

Converts Standard Coordinates on tangent plane to RA and DEC on the sky. data dictionary must also contain the CD matrix. Full equations: xi = cdmatrix(0,0) * (x-crpix(0)) + cdmatrix(0,1)* (y - crpix(1)) eta = cdmatrix(1,0) * (x-crpix(0)) + cdmatrix(1,1)* (y - crpix(1)) then ra = atan2(xi, cos(dec0)-eta*sin(dec0)) + ra0 dec = atan2(eta*cos(dec0)+sin(dec0),

sqrt((cos(dec0)-eta*sin(dec0))**2 + xi**2))
Parameters:(dictionary) (data) – should contain standard coordinates X, Y,

RA and DEC of the centre point, and the CD matrix.

SamPy.astronomy.conversions.angularDiameterDistance(z, H0=70, WM=0.28)[source]¶

The angular diameter distance DA is defined as the ratio of an object’s physical transverse size to its angular size (in radians). It is used to convert angular separations in telescope images into proper separations at the source. It is famous for not increasing indefinitely as z to inf; it turns over at z about 1 and thereafter more distant objects actually appear larger in angular size.
